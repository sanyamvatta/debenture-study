Valid Private Placement

You are within the limits of Section 42. A Private Limited company can issue debentures to a maximum of 200 persons in a financial year.

Investor Cap Utilization

Why Debentures?

Debentures are essentially loans taken by the company from the public (or specific individuals). Unlike shares, they do not carry voting rights.

  • 1 Zero Dilution: You mentioned 3 partners with 33% each. Issuing debentures ensures your ownership percentages remain exactly the same.
  • 2 Control: Debenture holders are creditors, not owners. They cannot interfere in daily management (unless you default).
  • 3 Tax Benefit: Interest paid on debentures is a tax-deductible expense for the company.

Post-Issuance Ownership Structure

Notice: Even after raising crores, the pie remains 33/33/33.

Secured vs Unsecured

You can issue Secured (backed by assets) or Unsecured debentures. Secured are more attractive to investors but require creating a charge on assets.

Convertibility

You can choose Non-Convertible (NCDs) or Convertible (CCD/OCD). Since you want to protect equity, choose Non-Convertible.

Tenure

Debentures for a Pvt Ltd usually cannot exceed 10 years (for general projects) or 30 years (infrastructure).

The "500 Person" Problem

Section 42 of Companies Act, 2013: A private placement offer can be made to a maximum of 200 persons in a financial year.

If you offer to 500 persons, it is deemed a Public Offer. Since you are a Private Limited company, you are legally prohibited from making a public offer. Doing so attracts heavy penalties (amount raised or 2 Cr, whichever is higher) and refund orders.

The Exclusions

The 200 limit excludes Qualified Institutional Buyers (QIBs) and Employees (under stock option schemes). However, for simple debentures, these exclusions rarely apply to friends/family.
